This experiment modulates the metacognitive capacity by synthetic cortisol (20 mg Hydrocortisone) vs. placebo, 20 minutes before the task.

******************************************************************
Task: Visual Metacognition (see Fleming et al., 2010), No Manipulation.

Confidence Scale: Continuous, 1 (sure I made an error) and 6 (sure I answered correctly)

Block Size:  300 trials in 5 Blocks 

Feedback: None

Subject population: 46 Chilean students (18-35)

Special Instructions: Respond in less than 2000 ms. 

******************************************************************
Data:

Subj_idx: The Subject Number
Stimulus: Stimulus Identity (1 = First Scene, 2 =  Second Scene)
Response: Subject's response (1 = First Scene, 2 = Second Scene)
Confidence: Subject's confidence rating (between 1 and 6)
RT_dec: The decision reaction time (RT in seconds)
RT_conf: The confidence reaction time (RT in seconds)
Contrast: The contrast varied during the experiment.
Condition: Drug 20 min after the experiment (P =  Placebo ; H = Hydrocortisone (20 mg)).
Accuracy: Performance 

Note: 3 subjects did not complete the 300 trials. In all cases there were technical problems (Subject 3 = 298 trials; Subject 34 = 181 trials; Subject 39 = 247 trials).
